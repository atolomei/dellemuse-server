Components of an audio guide

An Audio guide includes an introductory audio for the exhibition and audio tracks for all or part of the artworks and objects in the exhibition. The audio can be generated and published in all the venue's languages.

exhibition audio
artwork audio

The audio guide can be of two types: General or Accessible ( icon ) and visitors can choose in the visitor guide which type of audio guides they consult.

Only audio guides with published status are accessible to visitors in the visitor guide. Those with deleted status will be automatically removed from the system, and those in Editing status are for working in the content management application. The icons are:

Editing
Deleted

Audio guide information

In the Audio Guide Information you enter the curatorial text in the venue's primary language, and you can upload the introductory audio for the exhibition or generate it automatically using Artificial Intelligence from the Audio Studio.

The audio icon in the dropdown menu indicates that audio already exists in those languages

Audio for the exhibition's artworks and objects

Audio tracks for the exhibition's artworks. This is a subset of the exhibition's artworks.

Audio for all or part of the exhibition's artworks. Those with the "Editing" icon are not visible in the visitor guide

Artwork audio

The artwork audio can be uploaded in the information form or generated from the Audio Studio. For the venue's secondary languages, the audio can be uploaded or generated with Artificial Intelligence, in which case the first step is translating the curatorial text from the primary language to the corresponding language — which can be done automatically in the form — and then opening the Audio Studio to generate it.

Artwork audio

QR code and audio number of the artwork

Each artwork has a QR code and an audio number generated by the system that must be printed and placed next to the artwork. The visitor scans the QR with their phone and accesses the artwork's audio. The audio number is another way to access the audio, through the search.
