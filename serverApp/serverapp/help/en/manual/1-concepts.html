Introduction

Dellemuse is a free service that enriches the cultural experience in museums and historic buildings through audio guides generated with the assistance of Artificial Intelligence that visitors can listen to on their mobile phones.

The museum or institution uses a simple web application where it uploads and manages its artworks and permanent and temporary exhibitions.

It can create audio guides for exhibitions and their artworks in several languages, with the option to generate the audio using Artificial Intelligence.

Each venue of each institution can publish a Visitor Guide, which visitors can use to access the audio guides. To do this, the QR code generated by the system must be placed at the entrance. Visitors scan the QR with their phone and enter the guide — no registration or app download is required.

In addition, each exhibition and artwork has its own QR code that visitors can scan to access the audio guide.

Institutions and Venues

The Institution is the museum or historic site. It has one or several Venues. Normally it has a single venue, but it can have several. For example, the Museo del Prado is an institution with a single venue, but the Museo de Arte Latinoamericano de Buenos Aires (MALBA) is an institution with two venues: the Buenos Aires City venue and the Escobar venue.

Each venue has its own collection of Artworks and Objects, and its own database of Artists.

Each venue also has the languages in which audio guides can be generated. There is a primary language and several secondary languages. The primary language is used across all content as the source for automatic translations to other languages. Audio guides can be generated automatically in the primary language and in any of the secondary languages.

Application language and audio guide language

It is important to distinguish between:

the language in which the user uses the management application
the languages of the content and audio guides.

The management application language is the language in which the texts of the management interface are displayed, such as buttons, labels, error messages, etc. Each user selects it in their profile. Currently the management application languages are Spanish, English and Portuguese.

The languages in which audio guides can be generated are: Spanish, English, Portuguese, French, Italian, German.

Statuses: published, editing, deleted

Content in the application can have the following status:

Published (visible in the visitor guide), Editing (visible only in the management application, not shown in the visitor guide), Deleted (in the recycle bin).

The visitor guide

Each venue has a Visitor Guide, which is a public portal with its logo where visitors access from their phone, simply by scanning the venue's QR code that must be printed at the entrance.

For the Visitor Guide to be accessible online, the venue must be in Published status. It must also be explicitly enabled from the venue's Visitor Guide page.

The venue's QR code is generated by the system and can be downloaded in PDF or PNG format.

The visitor guide displays only the exhibitions and audio content of exhibitions and artworks with Published status.

Each exhibition can have one or more audio guides. Each audio guide consists of an introductory audio of the exhibition and audio tracks for some or all of the exhibition's artworks.

Visitors can navigate the application and access content in three ways: by browsing the application, by scanning the QR code of an artwork or exhibition, or by using the search to find content by audio number. The QR code of each artwork or exhibition and the audio number are generated by the system and can be downloaded in PDF or PNG format.

Exhibitions

Each venue has its own exhibitions, which can be from the permanent collection or temporary.

Each exhibition includes Artworks and Objects on Display, which must be selected from the venue's archive.

Audio guides

An Audio guide includes an introductory audio for the exhibition and audio tracks for all or part of the artworks and objects in the exhibition. The audio can be generated and published in all the venue's languages.

The audio guide can be of two types: General or Accessible for people with reduced vision ( icon ), and visitors can choose in the visitor guide which type of audio guides they consult.

Only audio guides with published status appear in the visitor guide. Those with deleted status will be automatically removed from the system after a period. The icons are:

Editing
Deleted

The audio number is used in the search, and must be added to the physical artwork or object on display so that visitors can use it when standing in front of the object and want to listen to the audio on their phone.
