General information

The first step after registering is to configure the venue on the General Information page of the venue.

Languages

This page is where you select the primary language of the Venue and the secondary languages. For example, at the National Museum of Fine Arts of Argentina (MNBA), the primary language is Spanish, and the secondary languages are English and Portuguese.

The Information form must be completed in the primary language of the Venue. For each secondary language the system creates a form with the required fields in that language. Automatic translation is performed by the system from the content in the primary language.

In the MNBA example, there is the Information (es) form for the primary language, and also Information (en) and Information (pt).

Time zone

The venue has a time zone that is used to calculate the validity of temporary exhibitions. For example, if the venue has the Buenos Aires time zone, and an exhibition has a start date of May 1st, the system will consider that exhibition to be active from midnight Buenos Aires time on May 1st.

Logo

You can also upload the venue's logo, which will be used in the Visitor Guide.

Archive

The Artworks and Objects archive of the venue belongs to that venue. Each one can be added to exhibitions (as an Exhibition Artwork). When adding an artwork, it is possible that the artist does not yet exist in the venue's archive, in which case you must go to the Artists section and add them before completing the artwork's editing.
